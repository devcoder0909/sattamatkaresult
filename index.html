History of Satta Matka

The history of Satta Matka dates to the 1960s when it originated as a form of gambling in Mumbai, India. Initially, it involved betting on the opening and closing rates of cotton in the Bombay cotton exchange. The game underwent several modifications and evolved into a numbers-based betting system. Players began betting on random numbers ranging from 0 to 9, which were drawn from a Matka (earthen pot). Along with time various Matka Markets came out known as Kalyan Matka, Mumbai Matka, Rajdhani Matka, Night Milan Matka, and Main Mumbai Matka, among others.

Types of Satta Matka

  • Kalyan Matka: One of the most popular variants of Satta Matka. They have their own rules of draws for the declaration of winning outcome.
  • Mumbai Matka: The format of satta matka associated with Mumbai. The game includes various draws which helps players to place bets on different numbers.
  • Rajdhani Matka: Involves betting on numbers based upon the opening and closing rates in the Rajdhani Cotton Exchange.
  • Night Milan Matka: Played at the evening or the night hours which offers exciting benefits to players looking to try their luck after sunset.
  • Main Mumbai Matka: Officially focused on Mumbai with their own sets of rules, draws, and betting options.
  • Gali Deshawar Matka: A specific variant popular in certain regions of India involving predicting the outcome based on the draw.
  • Milan Day Matka: A daytime variant providing players with betting opportunities during the day.

The Basics of Matka

The Satta Matka games generally include playing bets on a set of numbers from 0 to 9 and the result is drawn randomly. Players need to place bets on the combination of numbers on the opening and closing times. The Matka Draw involves drawing numbers from 0 to 9 from a deck of cards twice to create a two-digit number. Payouts vary depending on the various types of bets placed and the amount.

Different Variants of Matka Games

  • Single: Bet on a single digit from 0 to 9.
  • Jodi: Choose two numbers from 00 to 99 to form a pair.
  • Patti: A three-digit number variant (single, double, and triple patti).
  • Half Sangam & Full Sangam: Combination bets selecting numbers from both sets.
  • Cycle Patti: Bet on a set of three numbers in a specific order.

Quick Satta Matka FAQ

What is Satta Chart Analysis?

The Satta Chart Analysis is the study of historical Satta Matka to identify patterns, trends, and number frequencies. It helps in making decisions for future bets.

What are Matka Open and Matka Close?

"Matka Open" is the first set of numbers announced at the beginning. "Matka Close" is the second set announced at the end of the game.

What are Matka Panna and Matka Jodi?

Matka Panna is a three-digit number representing a panel, whereas Jodi represents a pair of two-digit numbers where players place bets.

What is Fix Satta?

Any predetermined or fixed set of numbers refers to Fix Satta. It is beneficial for players who have access to the information as the outcomes are pre-decided.
